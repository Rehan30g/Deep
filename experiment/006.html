Journal Entry: Experiment 006

Project: Canine Telepathy Initiative

Objective: To achieve telepathic communication between humans and canines, allowing a direct mental link for understanding and controlling animal behavior.

Experiment 006 commenced in 2005 with ambitious goals. By splicing canine DNA into human neural systems, I sought to enable telepathic communication between man and dog. At first, the idea seemed harmless—a revolutionary breakthrough that could alter how humans interacted with the animal kingdom, starting with our most loyal companions.

The first subject, designated 006-A, underwent a series of genetic infusions. Within days, we saw early signs of success. 006-A began to sense thoughts from the test dogs in the lab. Their emotions, needs, even basic instincts were transferred directly into his consciousness. "I can hear them," he reported with an unsettling clarity. "It’s like they’re speaking inside my mind."

However, by the second week, complications arose. 006-A started hearing not just individual thoughts but a cacophony of canine desires—hunger, territorial instincts, fear, loyalty—all flooding his mind without pause. He could no longer differentiate between his own thoughts and the overwhelming noise of the animals' minds.

As the mental link grew stronger, it became apparent that 006-A was losing his sense of self. He communicated less with the research team, and his speech devolved into fragmented sentences, mimicking the simple thought patterns of the dogs. By the third week, he struggled to form coherent sentences. "They... want to run... to hunt... I must... follow."

The situation worsened when 006-A’s behavior became more animalistic. He stopped using utensils to eat, began sleeping on the floor, and even attempted to assert dominance over the other members of the research team. His mind had been overtaken by the instincts of the dogs he had connected with telepathically. The once-promising subject was now ruled by primal urges, with little of his human consciousness remaining.

Despite our best efforts to reverse the process, 006-A became increasingly erratic and dangerous. He exhibited heightened aggression, especially towards anyone he perceived as a threat to the pack of dogs. It was clear that his brain had undergone irreversible changes—he had become more beast than man.

Ultimately, I was forced to terminate the experiment. 006-A had lost the ability to communicate in human language entirely, reduced to a being driven by animal instincts. He no longer recognized himself as human. The dogs' minds had fused with his own in a twisted symbiosis.

The Canine Telepathy Initiative proved to be a catastrophic failure. What was meant to connect human and animal in harmony instead erased the boundary between them, leaving only chaos in its wake. This experiment has made one thing clear: some barriers between species are not meant to be broken.
